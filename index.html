About Us

FORWAC is a nonprofit, non-governmental organisation, registered in Kenya in 1997 to address the plight of impoverished rural communities. Especially that of women and children, who stand out as the most vulnerable.

FORWAC is active in Ulamba sub-location, North East Gem location in Siaya County, which is situated forty kilometres west of Kisumu, on the shores of Lake Victoria.

Here, in Ulamba, HIV / AIDS has had a very strong and devastating impact:

  • Siaya County has one of the highest prevalence rates in Kenya.[1][2][3]
  • Many people live on less than $1 per day.[4]
  • Many school age children have lost one or both parents to the scourge.

How May I Help?

FORWAC’s main source of funding is through our sister organisation, FORWAC Germany, friends, relatives and well wishers who find it worthwhile to assist in putting a smile on the faces of these vulnerable children.

Some students, whom FORWAC sponsored through secondary school, are currently pursuing further studies, in Kenya's national universities, and in various middle colleges.

You can help a child stay in, or join, a class in secondary school, primary school or even nursery school.

Get Involved

FORWAC supports at least 90 students every year.

However, there are many on the waiting list, who have either not joined school, or are in and out of school.

The following are typical annual fees:

Primary/elementary education, fees, uniform and books. KES 12,000 (USD 120).

Secondary/high school education, fees, uniform and books. KES 22,000 (USD 220).

University/college education, fees and accommodation. KES 60,000 - 70,000 (USD 600 - 700).

By so doing, you give a poor child a smile and a future.
